# 📖 Project Overview

The **Wireless EV Charging Station using Solar Energy** is an innovative charging system that combines renewable solar energy with wireless power transfer (WPT) technology to charge Electric Vehicles (EVs) without using physical charging cables.

With the increasing adoption of electric vehicles, conventional wired charging methods present challenges such as cable wear, safety risks, maintenance costs, and user inconvenience. This project aims to overcome these issues by implementing **Inductive Power Transfer (IPT)**, allowing energy to be transferred wirelessly through magnetic fields.

In **Phase 1**, the project focuses on designing and implementing the transmitter and receiver circuits, studying wireless power transfer, analyzing charging efficiency, and validating the feasibility of solar-powered wireless EV charging.

# ❓ Problem Statement

Electric vehicles are becoming increasingly popular, but existing charging systems rely heavily on physical charging cables. These cables experience wear and tear, require regular maintenance, and may pose electrical safety risks.

Additionally, conventional charging stations consume electricity from the grid, increasing dependency on non-renewable energy sources.

This project proposes a **Solar Powered Wireless EV Charging Station** that utilizes inductive coupling technology to wirelessly transfer energy from a charging pad to an EV, thereby improving convenience, safety, and sustainability.

# ⚙️ Working Principle

The working of the wireless EV charging station consists of the following steps:

1. The solar panel converts sunlight into electrical energy.
2. The generated energy is stored inside the Li-ion battery.
3. The transmitter circuit converts DC power into high-frequency AC.
4. The transmitter coil generates an alternating magnetic field.
5. The receiver coil captures the magnetic field through inductive coupling.
6. The receiver circuit rectifies AC into DC.
7. The regulated DC output charges the EV battery wirelessly.

This eliminates physical charging cables while maintaining efficient power transfer.

# 📑 Synopsis

Electric Vehicles (EVs) are rapidly replacing conventional fuel-based transportation systems. However, existing charging infrastructure still depends on wired charging, which introduces several practical limitations such as cable damage, electrical hazards, and user inconvenience.

This project presents a **Wireless EV Charging Station powered by Solar Energy** using **Inductive Power Transfer (IPT)** technology. The system transfers electrical energy without physical contact between the charging station and the electric vehicle.

The transmitter circuit converts DC energy into high-frequency AC, generating an alternating magnetic field through the transmitter coil. The receiver coil captures this magnetic field and converts it back into DC power to charge the EV battery.

Solar panels provide renewable energy for the charging station, reducing dependence on conventional electricity sources and promoting environmentally friendly transportation.

Phase 1 primarily focuses on the circuit design, hardware implementation, testing, and analysis of the wireless charging system.
